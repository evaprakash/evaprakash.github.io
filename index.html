Eva Prakash

Hi! I'm Eva Prakash, a graduate student at Stanford completing my B.S./M.S. in Computer Science on the Artificial Intelligence Track and Creative Writing Minor on the Prose Track. I'm a research assistant at the Stanford Center for Artificial Intelligence in Medicine and Imaging and Stanford Machine Learning Group working with Professors Andrew Ng and Curt Langlotz on medical vision-language models. Previously, I was a research intern in the Stanford Vision and Learning Lab, Stanford Partnership in AI-Assisted Care, and Stanford Artificial Intelligence Lab, where I worked with Professors Fei-Fei Li and Anshul Kundaje. At Stanford, I have also been a graduate course assistant for CS 231N and CS 221.

Research

Broadly, my research focuses on utilizing computer vision and natural language processing techniques in the medical space. Some of my specific interests include multimodal models for clinical applications, generative models for synthetic medical data, radiology report generation, interpretability for machine learning models, and bias and uncertainty in vision-language models.
